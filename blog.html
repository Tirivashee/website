The Ballylike Journey: From Vision to Vibe

Brand Story / October 16, 2025 • by Ballylike

The Ballylike Journey

In a world full of noise and fleeting trends, Ballylike represents calm confidence — a modern Zimbabwean lifestyle brand built on faith, creativity, and authenticity. But before it became a name recognized for minimalist designs and handmade tote bags, Ballylike began as a simple idea in a high school classroom.

The Beginning

“The slang word Bally was introduced to me at my high school,” says Tirivashe Chitanda, founder of Ballylike. “The meaning resonated with my character, mentorship, and creativity in growth and faith.”

The name Ballylike grew from his way of doing things — in an exceptional, standout way. “I did my art in a Ballylike way,” he explains. The word blends the British slang “bally,” used for emphasis, with mentor-like, legendary qualities — a perfect reflection of his artistic mindset.

From day one, Tirivashe dreamed beyond limits: “I saw Ballylike in different countries and cities, engaging with people, and seeing the dream get bigger. I’m super grateful for where I am now — and I’m still working hard to keep moving forward.”

Vision & Values

Ballylike’s mantra, Faith. Create. Grow., forms the foundation of everything it stands for.

Faith means finding it within yourself to imagine, dream, and believe your vision into existence. Create means taking action — being a slave to your dream. And Grow represents evolution, turning potential into reality.

“It gives me hope and reminds me that I only live once. It can’t be for no reason. So let’s run Ballylike like no man’s business. Let’s run it the Ballylike Way.”

The Creative Journey

The journey from digital art to fashion was born out of curiosity and courage. “I was inspired by a tote bag design I made for a German bookshop,” he recalls. “I woke up in the middle of the night, started sketching, and told my dad at 5 a.m. we needed to go find fabric in town.”

They didn’t find the exact one he imagined — but they found inspiration in the process. “Each fabric led to a new design,” he smiles. “I started small — one fabric, one design at a time.”

Minimal. Urban. Confident. That’s how Ballylike’s style can be described.

Challenges & Growth

Balancing school, power cuts, and limited resources wasn’t easy. “I had to learn money management young — investing in myself like I didn’t have parents. We were rich in spirit and mental wellness, but I wanted more.”

“I feel like giving up every day,” he admits. “That’s why I wake up and prove myself wrong — because I’m arrogant by nature.”

“Wealth is energy. You have to master it like you possess it.”

Marketing & Identity

Ballylike designs for the modern Zimbabwean creative — people who move with intention, travel, attend classes, church events, and live with quiet confidence. They value style without shouting for attention.

Instagram (@ballylike_shop) tells the emotional story — visual, lifestyle-focused, and authentic. Each photo feels curated and calm. The website (ballylike.co.zw) completes the experience — minimalist, local, and easy to navigate. Together, they make Ballylike feel both homegrown and world-class.

“Instagram builds the emotion. The website delivers the experience.”

Standing Out

Ballylike stands apart by blending modern Zimbabwean identity with global polish. Instead of loud prints, it embraces minimalism and quiet confidence. Every design, photo, and post reflects intention and calm sophistication.

It’s a voice for a new generation of Zimbabweans — creative, grounded, and globally minded. Ballylike proves that simplicity can be both powerful and beautiful.

“Simple. Soulful. Sophisticated.”

The Future

Tirivashe envisions Ballylike reaching new corners of Zimbabwe — from Harare to Vic Falls and Bulawayo — and crossing borders into South Africa and beyond. When asked about dream collaborations, he smiles, “SALT and Rachel Chinouriri. Their vibe aligns perfectly with Ballylike’s soul.”

Personal Reflection

“Ballylike made me more arrogant for positivity — stronger and more aware of my gifts,” he reflects. “Fear blocks clarity. Courage creates a great story. And a great story needs clarity.”

“You don’t need to be loud to be seen. Confidence is calm.”

Tirivashe Chitanda

Founder & Creative Director, Ballylike

"Creating not just for aesthetics — but for meaning, impact, and the next generation of dreamers."
